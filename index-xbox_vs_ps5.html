Xbox Vs PlayStation

The Xbox Series X is $499, it's Optical Drive is 4K UHD Blu-Ray Drive, it's 16 GB GDDR6 RAM, it's got a Memory Bandwidth of 10GB at 560GB per second, the CPU has 8x Zen 2 Cores at 3.8GHz (3.6GHz with SMT) while the GPU has Custom AMD Radeon RDNA Navi 12 Teraflops, 52 CUs at 1.825GHz - (Supports DirectX Ray Tracing). Video Output shows Native 4K, 8K Support, Up to 120hz & it's Peak GPU Shader Throughput does Variable Rate Shading (prioritizing individual shading). Their Data Transfer Speed (I/O Throughput) 2.4gB per second (Raw), 4.8GB per second (Compressed), it also has an impressive 1 TB NVMe SSD Storage Drive & an equally impressive External Storage Seagate Proprietary External 1TB SSD Expansion Card, USB 3.2 HDD Support. It also has full Backwards Compatibility for the Xbox 360, Xbox One, & Accessories. Their Streaming Game Service is Project xCloud, they also support multiple games at once so you can Quick Resume at any time. It also supports USB in Front (One Type-A USB Port) & Rear (1TB External SSD Slot) & lastly the A/V Hookups have Native 4K, 8K Support, 2 HDMI 2.1 Ports.

The PlayStation 5 is also $499, it's Optical Drive is 4K UHD Blu-Ray Drive (Digital Edition excluded, it's 16GB GDDR6 RAM (256-bit) RAM, it's got a Memory Bandwidth of 448GB per second, the CPU has 8x Zen 2 Cores at 3.5HGz while the GPU has Custom AMD Radeon RDNA Navi 10.28 Teraflops, 36 CUs at 2.23GHz - (Supports Ray Tracing and 3D Audio via Tempest Engine). Video Output shows 4K, 120hz refresh rate, 8K Support. Their Data Transfer Speed (I/O Throughput) 5.5GB/S (Raw), 8-9GB/S (Compressed), it also has an impressive Custom 825GB SSD Storage Drive & an equally impressive External Storage NVMe SSD Slot, USB HDD Support. It also has full Backwards Compatibility for the PlayStation 4 only. Their Streaming Game Service is Remote Play & lastly the A/V Hookups have 8K Support.
